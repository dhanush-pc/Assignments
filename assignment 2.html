History of laptops

Dynabook Concept (1968) – American computer scientist Alan Kay envisioned a portable computer called the Dynabook, designed for children. Though it was never built, it influenced future laptop designs
Osborne 1 (1981) – The Osborne 1 was the first commercially available portable computer. It had a 5-inch screen, weighed 24 pounds, and cost $1,795
Grid Compass (1982) – Designed by William Moggridge, the Grid Compass was one of the first clamshell-style laptops. It was used by NASA in space missions
Grid Compass (1982)IBM 5155 Portable PC (1984) – IBM released its first portable computer, the IBM 5155, which was bulky but introduced portability to business computing
Compaq LTE (1989) – The Compaq LTE was one of the first true notebook-style laptops, featuring a built-in hard drive and a compact design
Apple PowerBook (1991) – Apple revolutionized laptop design with the PowerBook, introducing a trackball and ergonomic keyboard layout, setting the standard for modern laptops
